Golden Parable
What is Golden Parable trying to do?

Golden Parable exists to help people define, craft, improve, and tailor their narratives.

We work with founders and companies to make sure the principles behind what they are building, and why, are well communicated.

This includes:

  • Defining the worldview a founder or company is expressing
  • Clarifying intention, not just content and messaging
  • Determining how to connect the narrative to action, so the story is lived and felt across product, culture, and brand

This is not just copywriting, B2B marketing, or sales.

Constructing narratives that actually shift perception, inspire belief, and create movement.

This work matters most when what’s being built is complex, abstract, or hard to explain.

Anti-slop

By slop, we mean generic, AI-flattened writing.

Language that sounds polished but says nothing.

Words assembled without thinking.

Golden Parable is intentionally anti-slop.

We believe writing is a craft.

It requires time, reading, refining, and care.

Clear language comes from clear thinking.

And clear thinking can never be automated.

Narratives are not ornamental

Most companies treat narrative as something ornamental.

Something layered on once the “real work” is done.

We don’t.

Narrative shapes how decisions are made,

how teams align,

and how ideas travel.

Sloppy language leads to sloppy thinking.

Careful writing creates coherence.

Why narratives?

Humans are social creatures.

Narratives are how information is stored and transmitted.

For early humans, stories were survival tools.

Knowledge moved through shared, communicated meaning around fires, across generations.

We are not machines.

We construct reality through patterns, interpretation, and causality.

Language doesn’t just describe the world.

It actively produces it.

Rhetoric matters.

It’s how ideas gain legitimacy.

Why narratives for founders and startups?

Regardless of what you’re building,

if you don’t have a narrative, you can’t disseminate your idea.

But that’s not the full picture.

Narratives aren’t just broadcasting tools.

They’re ideation tools.

They help you clarify:

  • What you believe
  • What you’re building toward
  • How your team makes decisions

A narrative becomes an internal and external compass.

It helps you locate the soul of what you’re building.

Companies are not just brands.

About stories (and storytelling)

Yes, stories matter.

Stories are how narratives move.

They are how ideas become legible, memorable, and sharable.

But Golden Parable is not focused on packaging something shiny

or retrofitting a story onto a finished product.

The real work happens before the story is told:

  • forming the narrative itself
  • refining the idea
  • shaping the worldview the company will eventually express

Storytelling without story-craft is decoration.

We care about the slow, unglamorous work of story-crafting.

On making ideas and worldviews move

In Fairy Tale (Fairy Tale of Kings), the exchange is deliberate and careful.

The king doesn’t dominate or conquer; he hands the world forward.

That gesture mirrors what successful companies must do.

Consciously transmit their internal worldview so it survives beyond its original stewards.

Golden Parable exists to make that transfer possible,

capturing a company’s invisible world

and ensuring it can be carried intact

from one holder to the next.

How Golden Parable approaches the work

We don’t list products or offerings.

We focus on the craft of writing.

Reading, writing, refining, and thinking carefully about language.

On reviving respect for rhetoric.

On resisting sameness.

On refusing AI-generated filler.

Less branding.

Less comms.

More meaning.

About Golden Parable

I’m Janya — a poet, data expert, and writer. Storytelling sits at the center of my work; it’s the thread that connects the two poles of my practice: poetry and data. Poetry moves the inner world, touching people on a visceral, emotional level. Data moves the outer world, shaping systems, decisions, and collective understanding. Together, they teach me how meaning travels — from what is lived to what is measured.

I hold a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science and International Relations from Tufts University, with a focus on technology, governance, and global systems. My background includes managing environmental and operational real-estate data for CBRE Investment Management’s $200B AUM portfolio, where I built and scaled large data systems used for emissions modeling, regulatory reporting, and portfolio-wide analysis.

I am the Head of Data and Engineering and Chief Storyteller at NuVert, an AI education company, building both the systems that power the organization and the stories that help people understand them.

I also write for Letterbrace, a technical literature company, and help founders and teams craft narratives through Golden Parable.
